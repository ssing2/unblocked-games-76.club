How to Play

Endless Truck features simple controls that are easy to learn:

  • Accelerate: Up Arrow, W key, or Right Arrow
  • Brake/Reverse: Down Arrow, S key, or Left Arrow
  • Balance Truck: Left/Right Arrows or A/D keys
  • Perform Stunts: Combinations of movement while airborne
  • Restart Level: R key or designated restart button
  • Pause Game: P key or ESC key

The goal is to drive as far as possible while collecting coins and fuel cans. Use the terrain to your advantage – hills become ramps for epic jumps, and proper landings help maintain momentum. Balance is crucial, especially during jumps and landings. The further you drive and the more stunts you perform, the higher your score!

Pro Driving Techniques

1. Master the "Perfect Landing" Technique

The difference between amateur and pro Endless Truck players comes down to one crucial skill: landing technique. When your monster truck goes airborne after a big jump, how you position it for landing can mean the difference between maintaining top speed and flipping over in a spectacular (but run-ending) crash. The "Perfect Landing" technique involves keeping your truck parallel to the ground when it makes contact. Use the Left/Right arrow keys (or A/D) to adjust your truck's rotation while in the air, aiming to have all four wheels touch down simultaneously. Start by making small adjustments rather than dramatic ones – subtle taps are usually all you need. The perfect landing not only prevents crashes but also conserves momentum, allowing you to maintain speed into the next section. Watch for the suspension compression on landing – a good landing will show all four shock absorbers compressing evenly. On particularly long jumps, you might need to make multiple adjustments as your truck's rotation changes throughout the jump arc. Some advanced players even use the throttle while airborne to fine-tune their truck's position – light acceleration can help level out a nose-down position, while briefly letting off the gas can help correct a tail-down angle. With practice, you'll develop an almost instinctive feel for keeping your truck level regardless of the jump's size or angle.

2. Develop "Fuel Efficiency" Strategies

Fuel management is a critical aspect of achieving long runs in Endless Truck, yet many players overlook strategic approaches to fuel efficiency. While collecting fuel cans is the obvious way to extend your journey, how and when you use that fuel makes an enormous difference. First, understand that your truck burns more fuel during heavy acceleration, especially when climbing hills or recovering from slow speeds. Maintain a steady throttle whenever possible rather than repeatedly mashing the acceleration. On flat sections, find your truck's "cruising speed" – the point where you're moving quickly but not maxing out the engine, which typically burns fuel most efficiently. When approaching downhill sections, ease off the gas and let gravity do the work, essentially getting "free" distance without burning precious fuel. For uphill climbs, build momentum before reaching the base of the hill so you can reduce throttle while still making it to the top. Some advanced players use a technique called "pulse driving" on flat sections – briefly accelerating to top speed, then coasting until speed drops slightly, then accelerating again. This can be more fuel-efficient than constant full throttle in some versions of the game. Always prioritize fuel cans that are easily accessible, but make calculated decisions about whether difficult-to-reach cans are worth the extra fuel spent obtaining them. Sometimes the most fuel-efficient path isn't a straight line, especially if a small detour leads to multiple fuel pickups.

3. Perfect the "Stunt Boosting" Approach

While driving far is the primary goal in Endless Truck, performing stunts isn't just for show – it's a strategic technique that top players use to maximize their scores and progress. The "Stunt Boosting" approach focuses on intentionally executing flips, rolls, and wheelies to earn bonus points and, in some versions, actual gameplay advantages like speed boosts or fuel bonuses. Front flips (achieved by approaching a jump with proper speed and then holding the backward direction key) are usually the easiest to control and land safely. Backflips (forward key during jumps) are riskier but often worth more points. For maximum points, focus on "clean" stunts – complete rotations that end with a perfect landing. A half-rotation that results in a crash is worth nothing. The timing of your stunt initiation is crucial – start your rotation too early, and you might not have enough air time to complete it; too late, and you won't get enough rotational momentum. Some advanced players chain multiple stunt types in a single jump – perhaps starting with a backflip and then transitioning to a barrel roll by switching from forward to left/right input mid-air. These combination stunts typically earn multipliers or bonus points. Look for natural stunt opportunities in the terrain – ideal stunt jumps have both a good launch point and a clear, flat landing area. Moderately upgraded suspension systems often provide the best stunt control, as they give you enough "bounce" for rotations while still allowing precise landings. Remember that successful stunts are about calculated risk – don't attempt complex maneuvers when you're low on fuel or in areas with limited landing zones.

4. Implement "Terrain Reading" Skills

The most successful Endless Truck players don't just react to the terrain as it appears – they actively read the landscape ahead and plan their approach accordingly. Developing strong "Terrain Reading" skills transforms random driving into strategic navigation. Train yourself to scan several seconds ahead of your truck, identifying key features like jumps, dips, climbs, and hazards. Different terrain elements require specific approaches: hills with rounded tops make excellent natural ramps when hit at the right speed, while sharp inclines might require momentum management to prevent unexpected flips. Look for visual cues that indicate terrain changes, such as color variations or texture shifts that might signal different surface types. Recognize "combination features" – like a downslope leading directly into an upslope – which create opportunities for massive jumps if approached correctly. Some areas include hidden paths or shortcuts that aren't immediately obvious but can save time and fuel. By proactively reading the terrain, you can adjust your speed and approach before reaching hazards, rather than reacting (often too late) when you're already on them. This forward-thinking approach is especially crucial for achieving long runs, as the terrain typically becomes more challenging the further you progress. Practice by deliberately focusing on the mid-distance during your runs, rather than staring at your truck itself. Work on identifying "rhythm sections" – sequences of bumps or jumps that can be navigated smoothly if you establish the right driving cadence. The most advanced players develop an almost intuitive understanding of how their truck's physics will interact with each terrain feature, allowing them to maintain speed through sections that would stop less experienced drivers.

5. Master "Momentum Conservation"

In Endless Truck, understanding and implementing "Momentum Conservation" techniques can dramatically extend your runs and improve scores. Unlike arcade racing games where you can instantly accelerate to top speed, Endless Truck's physics engine rewards players who intelligently preserve their vehicle's momentum. The fundamental principle is minimizing unnecessary speed losses – every time you need to rebuild lost speed, you're burning extra fuel and losing time. Approach hills with sufficient speed to crest them without slowing down; this often means accelerating before the incline rather than on it. When landing jumps, aim for downward slopes whenever possible – landing on an incline that matches your trajectory allows your truck to absorb the impact while maintaining forward velocity. Use the natural terrain contours to your advantage, following the path of least resistance when possible. Some advanced players use a technique called "pumping" on rolling terrain – slightly easing off the throttle at the crest of small hills and then reapplying it on the downslope, similar to how BMX riders pump through rollers. For sharp turns, reduce speed slightly before the turn rather than during it, allowing you to accelerate out of the turn and maintain better overall momentum. When approaching obstacle sequences, plan your route to minimize speed-killing impacts or harsh direction changes. One counterintuitive technique is occasionally accepting a smaller jump rather than a massive one if the smaller option allows for better landing conditions and momentum preservation. The true masters of momentum conservation develop a "flow state" where their truck seems to effortlessly glide through the terrain, rarely coming to a complete stop and always carrying just the right speed for each section – achieving this state requires practice, terrain reading skills, and an intuitive understanding of your truck's physics properties.

6. Implement "Strategic Upgrade Prioritization"

The upgrade system in Endless Truck offers numerous opportunities to enhance your vehicle, but knowing which improvements to prioritize can make an enormous difference in your progression. Rather than randomly spending coins on whatever upgrade is cheapest, develop a targeted upgrade path based on your playing style and current challenges. For beginners, engine and fuel tank upgrades often provide the best initial value – improved engine power helps navigate difficult terrain, while a larger fuel tank gives you more room for error in fuel management. As your skills improve, suspension upgrades become increasingly valuable, providing better landing control and stability during stunts. Tire upgrades typically improve traction, making it easier to accelerate on loose surfaces and maintain control on steep inclines. Players who focus on distance records should prioritize fuel efficiency upgrades, while those who enjoy stunt performance might focus on suspension and handling improvements. Some versions feature special ability upgrades like temporary boost or improved stunt controls – these should generally be saved for later in your progression when you have the skill to use them effectively. Be strategic about complete truck replacements versus incremental upgrades – sometimes saving for a new model with better base stats is more efficient than fully upgrading a lower-tier vehicle. Pay attention to upgrade synergies – certain combinations provide compound benefits when paired together, like coupling improved suspension with better tires for optimal off-road performance. The most efficient approach often involves focusing on one upgrade path to completion rather than distributing coins across multiple partial upgrades. Keep track of where you typically struggle during runs, and prioritize upgrades that address those specific challenges – if you frequently run out of fuel, focus on fuel capacity or efficiency; if control during jumps is your weakness, invest in suspension and handling.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I perform flips and stunts?

Performing stunts in Endless Truck is all about using the physics engine to your advantage through proper jump execution and mid-air control. The basic principle is simple: you need sufficient air time and rotational momentum. For front flips (where your truck rotates forward), approach a jump with good speed, then as you leave the ground, press and hold the down arrow or S key. This tilts your truck's nose down, initiating a forward rotation. For backflips, follow the same approach but press the up arrow or W key while airborne to pitch your nose upward, creating backward rotation. Barrel rolls (side flips) are performed by holding the left or right arrow keys (A or D) during a jump, causing your truck to rotate sideways. The key to successful stunts is timing – press the direction too early, and you might alter your jump trajectory; too late, and you won't get enough rotation. Jump height and distance significantly impact which stunts are possible – larger jumps provide more air time for complex or multiple rotations. Your truck's suspension upgrades affect stunt potential dramatically, with better suspension systems allowing more controlled rotations and landings. Some advanced players combine multiple inputs to create combination stunts, like starting with a front flip and transitioning to a barrel roll mid-air by switching from down to left/right input. The most important aspect of stunting is landing – an incomplete rotation that ends with your truck upside-down or on its side means a crashed run, regardless of how impressive the stunt looked. Practice on smaller jumps before attempting complex maneuvers on massive launches, and remember that different truck types have varying stunt capabilities based on their weight, suspension, and aerodynamics. With practice, you'll develop an intuitive feel for exactly when to initiate rotations and how long to hold inputs to complete clean stunts consistently.

Why do I keep crashing on landings?

Landing crashes in Endless Truck typically stem from several common issues that can be systematically addressed to improve your success rate. The most frequent cause is improper landing angle – your truck needs to be approximately parallel to the ground when all four wheels make contact. If your front wheels touch first (nose-down position), you'll typically flip forward; if your rear wheels contact first (nose-up position), you'll usually flip backward. This happens because the physics engine realistically simulates the leverage these impacts create on your truck's center of gravity. To correct this, focus on using directional controls during the final moments of your jump to level out your truck – if you're rotating forward, tap the backward control briefly; if rotating backward, tap forward. Another common issue is excessive speed for the landing zone – even with perfect angle control, landing at extreme velocities on certain terrain types can overwhelm your suspension system and cause crashes. Adjust your approach speed based on the landing area's characteristics. Jump height also impacts landing success significantly – extremely high jumps create more forceful impacts that require more precise angle control and better suspension upgrades. Some players struggle with landing on inappropriate surfaces – aiming for flat, smooth areas will dramatically increase your success rate compared to landing on obstacles, steep inclines, or irregular terrain. Different truck types have varying landing characteristics – heavier vehicles typically need more precise angle control, while those with upgraded suspension systems can absorb more landing imperfection. If you're consistently crashing after stunts, try simpler rotations or ensure you have sufficient air time to complete the stunt and level out before landing. Remember that suspension upgrades directly improve landing forgiveness – if landing issues persist despite technique improvements, investing in better suspension might be necessary. Finally, practice deliberate landings on smaller jumps before attempting complex maneuvers or massive air, as the fundamental mechanics are the same regardless of jump size.
