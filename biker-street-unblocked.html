How to Play

Biker Street features simple controls that are easy to learn:

  • Acceleration: Press Up Arrow or W to accelerate
  • Brake: Press Down Arrow or S to slow down
  • Lean Left: Press Left Arrow or A to move left
  • Lean Right: Press Right Arrow or D to move right
  • Wheelie: Press Space while accelerating to pop a wheelie
  • Jump: Hit ramps at high speed to perform jumps
  • Stunt: Press combination keys during jumps for stunts (varies by version)
  • Nitro Boost: Press Shift to activate nitro (if available)

The goal is to race through streets while avoiding obstacles, collecting coins, and completing level-specific objectives. Some levels might require you to reach a destination within a time limit, while others focus on collecting a certain number of coins or performing specific stunts.

6. Nitro Boost Management

Bike Types and Upgrade Strategies

Speed Demons

These motorcycles prioritize top speed over all other stats, making them ideal for experienced players who can navigate traffic with precision. Speed bikes shine on levels with long straightaways and minimal tight turns. When upgrading a speed demon, focus first on handling improvements to compensate for their main weakness, then push acceleration to help you recover quickly after necessary slowdowns. These bikes are high-risk, high-reward options â€“ you'll crash more often while learning to control them, but once mastered, they can achieve the highest scores. Best for: Players who value pure speed and have quick reflexes.

Balanced Cruisers

The jack-of-all-trades option, balanced bikes offer moderate performance across all stats. They're forgiving for beginners but still capable of impressive performance in the hands of skilled riders. When upgrading cruisers, identify what gameplay aspect gives you the most trouble and focus upgrades there first. If you frequently crash, boost handling. If you struggle to accelerate after turns, improve acceleration. These bikes adapt well to all level types and are perfect for daily challenges where you don't know what to expect. Best for: New players or those who enjoy versatility.

Nimble Handlers

These bikes sacrifice some top speed for exceptional handling and maneuverability, making them ideal for urban environments with frequent turns and dense traffic. Their responsive steering allows for last-second adjustments that would send other bikes crashing. When upgrading handling-focused bikes, boost their acceleration first to help them maintain momentum through complex traffic patterns, then gradually improve top speed. These motorcycles excel in technical sections and are perfect for coin collection challenges due to their precision. Best for: Players who prefer technical skill over raw speed.

Stunt Performers

Specialized for aerial tricks and jumps, stunt bikes have enhanced stability in the air and usually come with bonus point multipliers for successful stunts. Their unique suspension systems allow for higher jumps and safer landings. When upgrading stunt bikes, focus on jump height and stability attributes first, then improve speed to help you reach ramps with enough momentum. These bikes are ideal for stunt-focused challenge levels and for players looking to maximize their score through trick combinations rather than pure racing. Best for: Creative players who enjoy the showmanship aspect of motorcycle riding.
