How to Play

Dinosaurs Merge Master features intuitive controls that make gameplay smooth and enjoyable:

  • Drag & Drop: Click/tap and drag dinosaurs to combine identical ones
  • Buy Eggs/Dinosaurs: Click/tap the egg or dino button to purchase new basic specimens
  • Collect Resources: Tap on resources that appear above dinosaurs to collect them
  • Unlock Space: Use your earnings to clear more habitat space
  • Purchase Upgrades: Click on the shop or upgrade button to improve your collection
  • Save Progress: Most versions save automatically, but check for manual save options

The goal is to discover all dinosaur types by merging identical creatures to create higher-level varieties. Each new dinosaur produces more valuable resources, allowing you to expand your habitats and progress faster!

Pro Merging Strategies

1. Master the "Five-Merge" Technique

While the basic game mechanics encourage merging pairs of identical dinosaurs, advanced players use the "Five-Merge" technique to maximize efficiency. Instead of immediately merging two dinosaurs when available, try to wait until you have five identical creatures. When you merge five at once, you'll receive two higher-level dinosaurs instead of just one, effectively giving you a free upgrade. This requires patience and strategic space management, but the payoff is enormous over time. To implement this technique, dedicate specific areas of your habitat to "staging" dinosaurs of the same type until you accumulate five. This approach is particularly valuable for higher-level dinos that take longer to obtain. For example, merging five level 4 Velociraptors gives you two level 5 dinosaurs, effectively saving you the resources needed to create an entire level 5 dino from scratch. While it might be tempting to merge immediately for quick gratification, the compound benefits of consistently using the Five-Merge technique can literally double your progression speed in the long run. The most successful Dinosaurs Merge Master players make this technique a core part of their strategy from the early game onward.

2. Implement Strategic Space Management

Habitat space is your most valuable resource in Dinosaurs Merge Master, especially in the early game before you've unlocked expansions. Effective space management can dramatically accelerate your progress. Instead of randomly placing dinosaurs wherever there's room, organize your habitat into functional zones. Create a "merging area" where you gather identical dinosaurs, a "production area" for your highest resource-producing dinos, and a "staging area" for creatures you're not currently focusing on. Always keep a few spaces open to accommodate new purchases or unexpected egg hatches. When space gets tight, prioritize merging lower-level dinosaurs first, as they provide less value per square. Another advanced technique is the "corner compression" method – place your highest-level dinos in the corners of your habitat where they can still produce resources but are out of the way of active merging areas. Some top players even use temporary "sacrifice" strategies where they sell lower-value dinosaurs during special events or opportunities to make room for more valuable acquisitions. Remember that proper space management isn't just about fitting more dinosaurs – it's about optimizing your habitat's layout to support efficient merging chains and maximize resource production from your most valuable prehistoric creatures.

3. Optimize Your Offline Earnings Strategy

Since Dinosaurs Merge Master continues generating resources even when you're not actively playing, developing an effective offline earnings strategy is crucial for maximizing progress. Before logging off, make sure your habitat is configured to generate maximum value while you're away. Position your highest-level dinosaurs with plenty of free space around them so their resource production isn't blocked. Invest in offline earnings upgrades early, as these provide compounding returns over the entire time you play the game. Some versions of Dinosaurs Merge Master include special dinosaurs with enhanced offline production abilities – prioritize discovering and upgrading these creatures. Another key technique is the "pre-logout merge" – right before closing the game, merge any pairs that are ready but leave "almost ready" combinations unmerged. This ensures you'll have immediate merging opportunities when you return, jumpstarting your next session. Calculate the optimal check-in schedule based on your offline capacity – if your habitat fills up with resources after three hours, try to check in around that timeframe to collect and reinvest. Some advanced players even set reminders for optimal collection times during long school days. Remember that while active play is more efficient for merging, optimized offline strategy is what allows you to make continuous progress despite limited playing time.
